Welcome to Cox's website! ⭐️


  Hello, my name is Trevor Cox. I'm a self-appointed creative, wannabe skater, and a Long Island wise guy. In 2015, I graduated from Roslyn High School in suburban purgatoria. After that, I went to UC Berkeley 🐻, where I majored in Computer Science. I also took a diverse selection of other courses to feed my intellectural hunger. After 3.5 years, I graduated and started working at a small startup called test.io, which was eventually acquired by EPAM Systems.

  Inspired by my uncle's service with the Peace Corps in El Salvador 🇸🇻, I applied to a volunteer teaching position in Kenya. Then COVID hit. The Peace Corps evacuated thousands of volunteers from dozens of countries, and my status was in limbo. I decided to seize this opportunity to do something I had always wanted to try: teach 4 year old tykes how to shred black diamonds ⛷️. I packed up my off-white 1999 Audi A6 station wagon and left the Bay Area for Sun Valley, Idaho 🌄.

  I worked as a ski instructor for two winters. I taught people age 3 to 63 the beauty and joy of skiing. In the summers, I would return to California to work as the head cook at a summer camp in the Sierra Nevadas 🏔. After two seasons in each locale, my itinerant life came to an end. Finally, in October 2022 I departed for Peace Corps service in Kenya 🇰🇪.

  For the next two years, I worked as a secondary school teacher in a rural Kenyan school, where I taught math, computers, and life skills. I learned Swahili, cooked ugali, and used a squat toilet 💩. I also collaborated with local partners to write and implement two grants, totaling about $18,000. This money was used to build a computer lab for our school 🖥 🖨 🖱 ⌨. For more details, see Newsletter no.9.

  This is a place for me to post my writings and mess around with code. It's a Frankenstein's monster of code from Stack Overflow, LLM generated code, and code blatantly taken from my friend Frank's website. Shoutout to him you can check him out at: fjlin.me.
